Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Timeline of Dr. Borlaug's Life

  • 1914 – Born in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1933 – Leaves his family farm to attend the University of Minnesota
  • 1935 – Works in the Civilian Conservation Corps during the Depression
  • 1937 – Graduates and joins the US Forestry Service
  • 1938 – Marries Margaret Gibson, returns to school inspired by Elvin Charles Stakman
  • 1941 – Rejected by military; works on malaria control and applied science for the war
  • 1942 – Earns Ph.D. in Genetics and Plant Pathology
  • 1944 – Moves to Mexico to develop disease-resistant wheat strains
  • 1945 – Discovers method to grow wheat twice annually, doubling yields
  • 1953 – Develops wheat that supplies 95% of Mexico's needs
  • 1962 – Brings high-yield wheat to India, preventing famine
  • 1970 – Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1983 – Aids seven African nations in increasing food yields
  • 1984 – Becomes a professor at Texas A&M University
  • 2005 – Advocates for genetically modified crops to meet food demand
  • 2009 – Passes away at age 95

"Borlaug's life and achievement are testimony to the far-reaching contribution that one man's towering intellect, persistence, and scientific vision can make to human peace and progress."
